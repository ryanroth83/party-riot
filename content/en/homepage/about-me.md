It all started with a broken dog. My younger sister and I had just come home from a party, and her favorite balloon animal popped. Seeing how disappointed she was, I decided to see if I could fix it. I started looking up tutorials to figure out how to twist the latex without it breaking, and I realized I actually really enjoyed the process.

What began as a way to fix a toy turned into a dedicated hobby. I spent hours teaching myself new shapes—from simple swords to more complex animals—and eventually, I started bringing my kit to the birthday parties we were already attending. After volunteering to make balloons for the other kids, I was surprised when parents started offering to pay me for my time.

Today, I’ve turned that hobby into a professional service. There is something really rewarding about taking a simple balloon and turning it into something that makes a kid’s day. Whether it's a small neighborhood gathering or a big birthday event, I love bringing a little extra creativity and a lot of smiles to the party.
